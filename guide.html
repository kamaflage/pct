Chapter 00 · Overview
What is the
PCT Translator?
A single-file web app that helps you build a parallel corpus between any two languages and use it to produce high-quality AI-assisted translations.

This tool was built to solve a real problem: many languages lack sufficient digital translation resources. The PCT Translator lets you systematically extract, align, and store parallel sentence pairs from bilingual source documents — then use those pairs to guide AI translation.

Everything runs in your browser. No server, no account, no internet required after the page loads. Your data is stored locally.

The Five Tabs

🔨
Builder
Upload HTML or PDF bilingual source files. The tool pairs them up and sends them to Alignment Studio.
📚
Corpus
Your growing library of verified EN↔target sentence pairs. Load, browse, search, edit, and delete.
📖
Phrases
Key vocabulary — religious terms, grammar patterns, proper nouns. Auto-injected into every translation prompt.
✍️
Translator
Paste English text, build a context-rich prompt, paste the AI response back, then approve segments.
✅
Queue
Completed translation sessions. Review, export as .txt, or save back to corpus.

The Workflow at a Glance

1
Upload source files
Go to Builder. Upload matched HTML pairs or two EPUBs. The tool auto-detects which file is EN and which is target language from filenames and content.
2
Align paragraphs
Alignment Studio opens automatically. Trim EN and target paragraph lists until they match, then accept.
3
Grow the corpus
Accepted pairs land in the Corpus tab. Repeat for more articles — your corpus grows over time.
4
Translate with AI
Paste English into the Translator. Build Prompt pulls matched corpus examples + phrases. Copy → paste into Claude/GPT/Gemini.
5
Review and save
Paste the AI response back. Enter Review Mode, approve segments, save to the Queue.
💡
First time? Start by loading a jw_corpus.txt file directly in the Corpus tab if you already have one. That immediately gives you examples for the Translator without needing to go through the Builder.

Chapter 02 · Alignment Studio
Pairing English
& Target Language
The Alignment Studio is the heart of the corpus-building process. It shows you extracted paragraphs side-by-side and lets you reorder and trim them until they match.

How It Works

When you push an article to corpus, the tool extracts paragraphs from both the English and target-language source. These rarely align perfectly — EN and target documents often have different numbers of paragraphs due to layout differences, footnotes, or section headers.

Your job is to remove paragraphs from either side until the counts match. Then you click Accept & Push.

PDT//Translator Alignment Studio
Mismatch EN: 8 · Target: 6
🇬🇧 English · 8 paragraphs
Jehovah deserves our obedience even when we don't understand the reason.✕
Jeremiah obeyed despite being puzzled by God's instructions.✕
Page header text — remove me✕
Christians strive to imitate Jeremiah's example.✕
🌾 Target · 6 paragraphs
Jehowa vedeent daut, daut wie am jehuarsom sent, uk wan wie nich vestonen.✕
Jeremia wia jehuarsom, wan hee uk nich vestonen kunn.✕
Wie aus Christen wellen krakjt soo jehuarsom sennen aus Jeremia.✕

Step-by-Step

1
Read both columns
Scan the EN and target paragraphs. They should tell the same story in the same order. Spot anything that doesn't belong — page headers, footnotes, navigation text.
2
Delete extra paragraphs
Tap the ✕ button next to any paragraph that doesn't have a match on the other side. If a paragraph is in the wrong row — for example EN row 5 matches target row 7 — drag the ⠿ grip handle on the left edge of the cell up or down to move it into place. Each column reorders independently. The count badge updates in real time.
3
Match the counts
When EN and target paragraph counts are equal, the pill changes from Mismatch to Match ✓ and the Accept button activates.
4
Accept & Push
Click ✅ Accept & Push. The pairs are written to your Corpus, phrases are auto-extracted, and the Studio moves to the next article in the queue.
💡
Quality tip Fewer, well-matched pairs are better than many misaligned ones. If an article has wildly different paragraph counts and you can't figure out what to delete, use ⏭ Skip — it's better than pushing garbage pairs.
⚠️
Order matters Paragraph 1 in English will be paired with paragraph 1 in the target language. Make sure you delete from matching positions — don't delete a real paragraph just to make numbers work out.

Searching and Filtering

Use the search bar to find pairs by English or target-language text. Article filter chips appear at the top once you have multiple articles — click one to show only pairs from that source.

Editing Pairs

Every pair has an ✏️ edit button. Use this to fix typos in the target-language side, or to update the English if it was misextracted from the PDF. Changes save immediately to local storage.

Adding Pairs Manually

Press + Add Pair in the top right. A modal lets you type an English sentence, its target-language translation, and the source article name. Useful for adding individual key sentences or corrections.

💡
The corpus improves every translation The Translator tab searches your corpus for sentences similar to what you're translating. More corpus = better context examples = better AI output. Aim for 200+ pairs before heavy translation use.
Chapter 04 · Phrases
Vocabulary &
Key Terms
The Phrase Library holds individual word mappings — religious terms, grammar patterns, proper nouns. These are injected into every prompt automatically.

Why Phrases Matter

Your target language may have specific vocabulary that AI models don't know well. The phrase library ensures that every translation prompt includes locked-in term mappings — so the AI can't guess wrong on words like Jehowa, Bieebel, or Fräd.

Confidence Levels

  • ✅ Verified — Confirmed by a native speaker. Always injected into prompts.
  • 🟡 Suggested — Auto-extracted or uncertain. Injected but flagged in the prompt.
  • 🔵 Auto — Extracted from corpus text automatically. Review before relying on these.

Adding Phrases Manually

Press + Add Phrase. Fill in the English word, its target-language equivalent, the category (Religious, Grammar, Common, or Proper Noun), and set the confidence. Native speaker confirmations should always be marked Verified.

💡
Auto-extraction happens on every Push to Corpus When you accept aligned pairs, the tool scans them for common word patterns using co-occurrence analysis and auto-adds candidates to the phrase library as "Auto" confidence. High-confidence matches (≥80%) appear in a Suggestions banner at the top of the Phrases tab — tap ✅ Add ≥80% to approve them all at once, or review individually. Review the rest periodically and upgrade the good ones to Verified.
Chapter 05 · Matcher
🧩 Phrase
Matcher
The Matcher is a word-level alignment tool. Pick any sentence pair from your Corpus, deconstruct both sides into individual word tokens, then tap to group and link words across languages. Every connection you confirm gets written directly to your Phrase Library as a verified entry.

Opening the Matcher

Go to the Corpus tab. Every sentence pair now has a 🧩 button alongside the ✏️ edit and ✕ delete buttons. Tap it — the Matcher tab opens instantly with that pair pre-loaded. No copy/paste needed.

PCT//Translator
CORPUS
English
Jehovah deserves our obedience even when we don't understand.
Target
Jehowa vedeent daut, daut wie am jehuarsom sent.
✏️ 🧩 ✕

How It Works

1
Tokens appear
Both the English and target sentences are split into individual word chips. English on top, target below. Each word is a separate tappable token.
2
Select tokens on both sides
Tap a word to select it (it highlights in accent colour). Tap more adjacent words to extend into a phrase — for example tap our then obedience to form the phrase "our obedience". Do the same on the target side for its equivalent.
3
Link ↔
Once you have selections on both sides, the action bar appears showing a preview: "our obedience" ↔ "wie am jehuarsom sent". Tap Link ↔ to confirm the pair. The tokens get colour-coded — pair 1 green, pair 2 blue, pair 3 gold, and so on.
4
Repeat for each phrase
Continue linking across the sentence. Structural words with no direct equivalent — articles, prepositions — can be left unlinked. The Linked Pairs list below the tokens shows everything you've connected so far. Tap ✕ on any linked pair to remove it.
5
Extract Phrases
Tap ⬇ Extract Phrases. Every linked pair is written to your Phrase Library as a Verified entry at 85% confidence. Duplicates are skipped silently. The tokens reset so you can load another pair.

Tapping a linked token

If you tap a token that's already linked, it unlinks that entire pair — both sides reset to neutral. This is faster than hunting for the ✕ button in the list below.

💡 Best use — The Matcher shines on pairs where the auto-extractor missed terms, or where you want to lock in proper nouns, religious terms, or multi-word phrases with full confidence. Even a few minutes per session builds the phrase library quickly.
Chapter 06 · Translator
The Translation
Workflow
The Translator tab is a structured three-step workflow: Write → Prompt → Review. It turns your corpus and phrases into a rich context package for any AI model.
1
Step 1 · Write — Paste your English text
Paste the English article text into the left pane. Press Analyze to run the pre-processor — it scans for matching phrases from your library and shows you what will be locked in. If nothing matches, a diagnostic toast tells you exactly why: empty library, corpus not yet pushed, or simply no matches in this text.

2
Step 2 · Prompt — Copy and paste into AI
Press Build Prompt →. The tool assembles a complete prompt including: your verified phrase mappings, the best-matching corpus example pairs, and the English text to translate. Copy it and paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini.

Model Picker

Tap the model indicator in the top bar to switch between Claude, ChatGPT/GPT-4, and Gemini. This doesn't connect to any API — it just adjusts the prompt wording to get the best results from each model's style.

💡
On mobile: The translator works as a single-pane stepped flow. Step 1 fills the screen, then Build Prompt → slides you to Step 2, and Enter Review Mode → takes you to Step 3. Use the back chevron ‹ to go back.

Exporting a Session

Press ⬇ Export .txt on any queue item to download a plain-text file with all approved EN↔target segment pairs. This format can be shared, printed, or used to populate other systems.

Clearing the Queue

The Clear Approved button in the top right removes all fully-approved sessions. Partially-approved sessions are left in place. Use this to keep the queue tidy after exporting.

ℹ️
Queue data is stored locally Everything in the Queue is saved in your browser's local storage. It persists between sessions on the same device, but won't transfer to another device unless you use the Export button.
Chapter 08 · Export & Tips
Saving Your Work
& Pro Tips
How to back up your corpus, move it between devices, and get the most out of the tool.

⬇ Export All

The ⬇ Export button in the top-right bar downloads a single pdt_export.json file containing your entire corpus, phrase library, and queue. This is your backup.

⚠️
Export regularly Your data lives in the browser. If you clear browser data, use private/incognito mode, or switch browsers, it's gone. Export after every significant session.

Moving to Another Device

Export on device A → transfer the HTML file and the JSON export to device B → open the HTML file → go to Corpus → Load .txt (or drag the JSON onto the corpus drop zone). Everything transfers.

Theme Toggle

The ☀️/🌙 toggle in the top bar switches between light and dark mode. Your preference is saved automatically.

Pro Tips

🎯
Match carefully
Delete spurious EN and target paragraphs before accepting. One bad pair misleads every future translation that matches it.
🔢
Sentence mode
In Review Mode, switch granularity to "Sentence" for better control on long articles. Paragraph mode is faster; sentence mode is more precise.
⛪
Religious terms first
Before translating any new topic, add any domain-specific terms to the Phrase Library as Verified. These are injected before the AI sees your text.
📋
PDF vs HTML
HTML source files from jw.org give cleaner paragraphs. PDFs work but may have extraction artifacts — always review in Alignment Studio before accepting.
💬
Analyze first
Always press Analyze before Build Prompt. It shows you which phrases matched — if your key terms aren't being caught, add them to the Phrase Library first.
🔄
Feedback loop
Good translations should go back into the corpus via the Builder or Add Pair. Your corpus grows and future translations get better automatically.
🌱
The goal: a living corpus This tool gets better the more you use it. Every pair you align, every phrase you verify, every translation you approve feeds back into better future prompts. Aim to treat the corpus as a long-term asset — not just a one-time import.
